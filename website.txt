    <section id="problem">
        <h2>The Problem</h2>
        <p>Stray animals pose serious challenges to our communities, including public health risks, overcrowded shelters, and increased costs for animal control. Diseases like rabies and parasites can spread from strays to humans, pets, and wildlife. Overwhelmed shelters often resort to euthanasia, creating moral and emotional dilemmas for staff and residents alike.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="solutions">
        <h2>Our Solutions</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Mandatory Spay/Neuter Programs:</strong> Prevent overpopulation by requiring spaying/neutering of pets.</li>
            <li><strong>Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR):</strong> Humanely manage feral cat populations through sterilization and vaccination.</li>
            <li><strong>Education Campaigns:</strong> Teach responsible pet ownership and the importance of adopting from shelters.</li>
            <li><strong>Incentives for Adoption:</strong> Provide tax benefits and subsidies for adopting or fostering stray animals.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>
